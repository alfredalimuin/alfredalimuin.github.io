SEO & Digital Presence
Interview Script

Role: Digital Presence Specialist · Focus: Google Visibility & Instagram Funnel

📹 Video Call 💪 Confident & Professional 🎯 Experienced Level
1
Opening Statement — First Impression
Say This First

When they say: "Tell me about yourself"

📣 Your Script
"I've built my experience around one core goal — helping businesses get found online and turning that traffic into real results. My focus has been on two things: making sure a website is visible where it matters most, whether that's Google search or AI-driven platforms, and using social media — particularly Instagram — as a deliberate traffic channel, not just a vanity metric. I enjoy the full picture, from the technical side of SEO all the way to crafting content that actually makes people stop scrolling and take action."
✅
Why This WorksIt immediately positions you as strategic, not just tactical. You're not someone who posts content — you're someone who drives results.
💡
Delivery TipPause after this. Let them respond. Confidence is knowing when to stop talking — don't rush to fill the silence.
2
SEO Approach — How You Think About It
Most Important

When they ask: "What's your approach to SEO?"

📣 Your Script
"My approach always starts with making sure the foundation is solid — the site needs to be fast, technically clean, and structured in a way Google can actually read. Once that's in place, I focus on content strategy. I look at what the target audience is genuinely searching for and build pages that answer those questions clearly and completely. I also think beyond traditional SEO now — optimizing for featured snippets and AI-generated answers is becoming just as important as ranking on the first page, so I factor that in from the start."
💡
Power Phrase to Use"I think beyond traditional SEO." — Say this confidently. It signals you're current and forward-thinking, not someone stuck in outdated tactics.
✅
Key Terms to Name DropGoogle Search Console, Core Web Vitals, featured snippets, schema markup, GEO (Generative Engine Optimization), AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)
Keyword Research Schema Markup Core Web Vitals Featured Snippets GEO AEO Search Console
3
Instagram Strategy — Driving Traffic
Social Focus

When they ask: "How do you use Instagram to drive traffic?"

📣 Your Script
"I treat Instagram as a warm-up channel, not a closing channel. The job of every post, reel, or story is to build enough curiosity or trust that someone wants to learn more — and then the website does the actual converting. So my content strategy is always built backwards from the funnel. I ask: what does this person need to believe before they're ready to take action? Then I create content that moves them toward that belief, with a clear path to the site. Engagement is important, but I always tie it back to traffic and conversions — otherwise it's just noise."
✅
Why This Answer Stands OutMost candidates talk about hashtags and posting schedules. You're talking about psychology and funnels — that's the difference between a content poster and a strategist.
⚠️
Avoid This MistakeDon't say "I focus on growing followers." Say you focus on qualified clicks and conversions. Followers don't pay bills — leads do.
Reels Carousels Bio Link Story CTAs Content Calendar Warm Audience
4
Connecting Traffic to the Funnel
Strategic Thinking

When they ask: "How do you connect traffic to the funnel?"

📣 Your Script
"The way I see it, traffic means nothing if the funnel leaks. So before I scale any traffic source, I make sure the funnel itself is solid — the landing page is clear, the call to action is obvious, and there are no unnecessary steps that cause drop-off. Once that's tight, I align every traffic source to that funnel. SEO brings in people who are actively searching. Instagram brings in people who are curious but not yet ready. Each channel needs different messaging, but they all lead to the same destination."
💡
Bonus PointMention "leak-proof funnel" if it's in the job description — it shows you read the brief carefully and understand the full picture, not just your piece of it.
✅
Key Terms to UseAbove the fold, CTA, landing page, conversion rate, traffic alignment, qualified traffic
Landing Page CTA Conversion Rate Above the Fold Traffic Alignment Funnel Stages
5
Measuring Results — What You Track
Data Driven

When they ask: "How do you measure your results?"

📣 Your Script
"I rely on data, not gut feeling. For SEO, I track keyword rankings, organic traffic, and click-through rates using Google Search Console and GA4. For Instagram, I look past likes and focus on profile visits, link clicks, and how many of those clicks actually convert on the site. The metric I care most about isn't traffic — it's qualified traffic. Are the right people landing on the right pages and taking the right actions? That's what tells me whether the strategy is actually working."
✅
Tool Name DropMentioning Google Search Console and GA4 by name immediately signals real hands-on experience — not just theoretical knowledge.
💡
Stat to MentionGoogle reports that a 1-second delay in page load time reduces conversions by 7%. Shows you connect technical work to business impact.
Google Search Console GA4 CTR Organic Traffic Link Clicks Qualified Traffic
6
Staying Current — SEO & AI Trends
Stand Out

When they ask: "How do you keep up with SEO changes?"

📣 Your Script
"SEO moves fast, so I make it a habit to stay ahead of it. I follow algorithm updates closely and I've been paying close attention to the shift toward AI-driven search — things like how ChatGPT and Gemini are starting to replace traditional search for a lot of queries. That's why I've started incorporating GEO — Generative Engine Optimization — alongside traditional SEO. The goal is to make sure content is optimized not just for Google rankings, but for wherever people are searching. And increasingly, that's AI platforms."
💡
Power MoveDropping "GEO" and "AEO" in this answer is a major differentiator. Most candidates won't know these terms — it immediately signals you're ahead of the curve.
⚠️
Don't Just Say"I read blogs and watch YouTube." Be specific — mention Google's Search Central blog, algorithm update tracking, and AI search trends.
GEO AEO AI Search Algorithm Updates ChatGPT Search Perplexity
7
Closing Statement — Leave an Impression
End Strong

When they ask: "Do you have any questions for us?"

📣 Your Script
"At the end of the day, this role is about making sure the right people find the business — and when they do, they actually convert. I'd love to understand the current state of the website and the funnel. Is the funnel already set up, or is that part of what I'd be building out? And on the Instagram side, is there an existing content library to work with, or would I be starting fresh? Understanding where things stand helps me know where I can make the biggest impact fastest."
✅
Why This Closes StrongYou connect technical work to business results — and your question shows you're already thinking like someone in the role, not just someone trying to get the job.
⚠️
Never Say This"No, I think we covered everything." — It makes you look passive and uninterested. Always have at least one smart question ready.
