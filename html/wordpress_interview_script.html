Interview Prep

WordPress Optimization
Interview Script

Role: WordPress Developer · Focus: Speed, Navigation & Conversion

1
Opening Statement — First Impression
Say This First
📣 Your Script
"When I look at a WordPress site that needs optimization, my first step is always a full audit before touching anything. I use tools like Google PageSpeed Insights and GTmetrix to get a clear picture of the speed score, then I look at the theme code, the plugins that are loading, and the overall page structure. From there I build a priority list — speed first, then navigation, then conversion elements — so every fix I make has a measurable impact."
✅
Why this worksShows you have a process, not just guesses. Interviewers love structure.
2
Site Speed — How You Fix It
Most Important
📣 Your Script
"For site speed, I tackle it in layers. First I install a caching plugin — either WP Rocket or LiteSpeed Cache depending on the hosting environment — because that alone can cut load time by 40 to 60 percent. Then I address images, which are usually the biggest culprit on real estate sites. I use a plugin like Smush or ShortPixel to compress and lazy-load every image so they only load when the visitor scrolls to them. Finally, I clean up the theme code — removing unused CSS, deferring JavaScript, and making sure no unnecessary plugins are loading on pages where they're not needed."
💡
Pro Tip — Mention This StatGoogle says a 1 second delay in load time reduces conversions by 7%. Shows you understand business impact.
✅
Key Tools to Name DropWP Rocket, LiteSpeed Cache, Smush, ShortPixel, Google PageSpeed Insights, GTmetrix
Caching Lazy Loading Image Compression CSS Minification JS Deferral Core Web Vitals
3
Navigation Fixes
UX Focus
📣 Your Script
"For navigation, my approach is to audit the menu structure from a visitor's perspective — not the owner's. I ask: can someone find what they're looking for in under 3 clicks? On a real estate site, the most critical pages are property listings and the contact or inquiry form. If those aren't front and center in the navigation, we're losing leads. I also check mobile navigation specifically because over 60% of real estate searches happen on mobile. If the hamburger menu is clunky or the dropdowns don't work on touch screens, that's an immediate fix."
💡
Bonus PointMention adding a sticky navigation bar so the menu follows the user as they scroll — keeps CTAs always visible.
⚠️
Avoid This MistakeDon't say you'd just "redesign the menu." Say you audit it first, then fix based on what the data shows.
3-Click Rule Mobile Navigation Sticky Header Hamburger Menu UX Audit
4
Conversion Optimization — Landing Page
Lead Gen
📣 Your Script
"For conversion optimization, everything on the landing page has one job — get the visitor to take action. For a real estate lead generation site, that action is usually filling out a contact form or clicking to view listings. So I make sure the hero section has a clear headline, a strong call to action button above the fold, and a simple form that doesn't ask for too much information upfront. I also add trust signals — things like testimonials, professional branding, and a photo of the agent — because people hire people they trust. And I make sure every page has only ONE primary call to action so visitors aren't confused about what to do next."
✅
Key Terms to UseAbove the fold, CTA, trust signals, form friction, hero section, single CTA focus
💡
Real Estate Specific WinMention adding a "What's My Home Worth?" tool prominently — it's a high-converting lead magnet for real estate sites.
Above the Fold CTA Button Trust Signals Lead Magnet Form Optimization Hero Section
5
Your Audit Process — Step by Step
Show Your Workflow
📣 Your Script
"My process always starts with looking at the site from the outside — I run it through Google PageSpeed Insights to get a speed score and a list of issues. Then I look at the page source code to identify what theme is being used, what plugins are loading, and what's bloating the page. From there I go into the WordPress admin to audit installed plugins, check for theme updates, and review the navigation settings. I build a priority list ranked by impact — quick wins first like caching and image compression, then deeper fixes like code cleanup and conversion layout changes. I always preview changes before they go live so nothing breaks on the real site."
✅
What This ShowsYou are methodical, careful, and business-minded. You don't randomly hack at code — you have a system.
6
Closing Statement — Leave an Impression
End Strong
📣 Your Script
"At the end of the day, WordPress optimization isn't just about making a site faster — it's about making sure every visitor who lands on the site has the best chance of becoming a lead. Speed keeps them from leaving. Good navigation helps them find what they need. And a well-optimized landing page gives them a clear reason to reach out. That combination is what turns a clunky website into a lead generation machine."
✅
Why This Closes StrongYou connect technical work to business results. That's exactly what clients and employers want to hear.
